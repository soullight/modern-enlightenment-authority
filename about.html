About Tantra Authority

Tantra Authority is published by The Naked Press, an imprint of LK Publishing, LLC, dedicated to laying out body-grounded practical knowledge that has historically been encrusted in mythology, marketing, or institutional gatekeeping.

What we publish

Articles on tantric practice — breath, sound, movement, touch, attention, sexuality, partnered work, solo work, the cultural context — written for the educated adult reader who is dissatisfied with the resolution at which most existing content addresses the subject.

We do not certify teachers. We do not run workshops. We do not sell weekend retreats. We do not have a private community you can pay to join. We publish writing, and we publish books. That is the entire business.

Our editorial position

  • Body-grounded. If a claim can't be verified by a reader in their own body within a reasonable time, we don't make it.
  • Anti-cult. We are explicitly hostile to the patterns that turn tantric teachers into gurus and tantric students into followers. Our orientation is to make every practice maximally extractable from any source, including ours.
  • Plain language. We use Sanskrit only when no English equivalent exists. We name physiological mechanisms when they explain what the practice is doing.
  • Adult-respect. We assume the reader is a competent adult capable of handling clear information about sexuality, anatomy, and the body. We do not soften, hedge, or moralize.
  • Anti-mythology. Where a practice works, we explain why it works. Where the cultural story around a practice doesn't hold up, we say so.

Our flagship book

The longer-form expression of this project is Beyond the Myth: The Definitive Guide to Modern Tantra, by Lawrence Lanoff, forthcoming from The Naked Press in 2026.

About The Naked Press

The Naked Press is the imprint Lawrence Lanoff founded to publish the Beyond the Myth book series — seven volumes covering tantric practice, energy work, open relationship structure, male and female sexuality, modern enlightenment, and sexual shadow. The work stripped of the mythology.

The companion publication is The Naked Mind, an essay newsletter on consciousness, sexuality, and the inherited frameworks that get in the way of both.
